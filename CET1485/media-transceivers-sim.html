CompTIA writes Objective 1.5 as "Compare and contrast transmission media and transceivers." That covers wired media (copper, fiber, coaxial, plenum vs non-plenum), wireless and cellular options, transceiver form factors (SFP/SFP+/QSFP+/QSFP28), and the eight connector types you have to recognize by sight. This simulation gives you live sliders to compare media side-by-side, a transceiver selector you can filter by port type, an interactive shared-vs-P2P demo, and ticket-style diagnostic scenarios — built around how the Net+ exam actually tests this objective.

Shared Media (Half-Duplex Hub)

One Collision Domain

All four stations share one collision domain. The hub repeats every signal out every port. Only one station can transmit at a time without a collision. Stations use CSMA/CD — listen first, transmit, detect collisions.

Always half-duplex. A station cannot send and receive simultaneously on shared media.

Point-to-Point (Full-Duplex Switch)

Four Collision Domains (one per port)

Each switch port is its own point-to-point link with its own collision domain. With full-duplex, each station has separate TX and RX pairs and can send and receive at the same time. No collisions possible on a full-duplex P2P link.

CSMA/CD is disabled on full-duplex interfaces. Modern Ethernet is essentially always P2P + full-duplex.

About this Simulation

An interactive Network+ exam prep simulation covering CompTIA N10-009 Objective 1.5 — compare and contrast transmission media and transceivers. Covers wired media (copper, fiber single-mode and multimode, coaxial, DAC), wireless and cellular options, 8 connector types tested by sight, and transceiver form factors (SFP, SFP+, QSFP+, QSFP28).

Built as a simulation rather than a drill: turn the distance and speed knobs in the Media Lab, watch the transceiver list update as you change ports, transmit on shared vs P2P to see CSMA/CD behavior, and use the reference panel to look up wireless standards, coax types, and connector identification.
