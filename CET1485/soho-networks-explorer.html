How to Pick

Speed alone is not the answer. Fiber is the obvious "best" — but it isn't available everywhere, and a 200 Mbps cable connection is plenty for a household streaming three 4K shows simultaneously. Match the connection to the use case.

Latency matters more than bandwidth for some workloads. A 1 Gbps satellite link with 600 ms round-trip will feel slower for video calls and gaming than a 50 Mbps DSL line at 20 ms. The exam likes to test this distinction.

Symmetric vs asymmetric is the upload story. Cable, DSL, and most cellular give you a fast download but a slow upload. Fiber typically gives you symmetric speeds — upload as fast as download — which matters for video conferencing, cloud backup, and hosting anything from home.

DHCP DORA Handshake

Four messages, two devices, one IP lease. This is how every laptop, phone, and smart bulb on your network gets its address.

Idle · client has no IP yet
1
Discover
Client broadcasts to all (255.255.255.255) — "any DHCP server out there?"
2
Offer
Server replies — "here's an IP you can have: 192.168.1.105"
3
Request
Client broadcasts — "I'll take that 192.168.1.105 lease"
4
Acknowledge
Server confirms — "it's yours. Lease: 24h. DNS: 8.8.8.8"

RULE OF THUMB: 2.4 GHz reaches farther but is crowded with everything from baby monitors to microwaves. 5 GHz is faster and cleaner but doesn't punch through walls as well. Modern dual-band routers serve both simultaneously and let clients pick.
Topic 02 · Channel Planning

2.4 GHz Channel Reality

Eleven channels in the US, each 22 MHz wide — but they overlap. Only three combinations don't step on each other:

CH 1
CH 6
CH 11
12345 67891011
EXAM PIN: Channels 1, 6, and 11 are the only non-overlapping channels in the US 2.4 GHz band. Picking channel 3 or 8 means you collide with two of those three.

5 GHz is easier — many more non-overlapping channels available, and channel bonding (40/80/160 MHz) gives you wider pipes when interference allows.

Topic 03 · Authentication & Encryption

Security Evolution

Open
Never
No encryption. Anyone can join, anyone can read packets. Coffee shops sometimes use this with a captive portal — but at the radio level it's still open.
WEP
Broken
RC4 stream cipher. Crackable in minutes with off-the-shelf tools. Don't deploy. If you find this on a network, change it the same day.
WPA
Deprecated
TKIP-based. A stopgap between WEP and WPA2. Don't deploy.
WPA2
Standard
AES-CCMP. The N10-008 baseline. PSK (pre-shared key) for SOHO, Enterprise (802.1X) for orgs.
WPA3
Newest
SAE handshake replaces PSK. Forward secrecy. Resistant to offline dictionary attacks. Listed on the N10-008 acronym list, prevalent on newer gear.

Exam Tips

· The SOHO router holds five jobs: routing, switching, NAT/firewall, DHCP, and Wi-Fi.

· 169.254.x.x means DHCP failed — the client self-assigned via APIPA.

· 1, 6, 11 are the only non-overlapping 2.4 GHz channels.

· Port forwarding is the answer when inbound connections need to reach a specific internal device.
