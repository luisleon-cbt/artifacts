Troubleshoot,
In Order.

Domain 5 (Troubleshooting) is 24% of the exam — the heaviest single domain — and Objective 5.1 is its foundation. Seven steps, in a specific order, that CompTIA expects you to follow. Skip a step, and the exam marks you wrong even if your diagnosis was technically correct. This drill walks every step, then drills the order, the sub-actions, and the most-tested anti-patterns until they're automatic.

The 7 Steps · In Order
1Identify the problem
2Establish a theory of probable cause
3Test the theory to determine cause
4Establish a plan of action
5Implement the solution (or escalate)
6Verify full system functionality
7Document findings, actions, outcomes
EXAM PATTERN
Most 5.1 questions ask "given step X, what's next?" or "this technician's action belongs to which step?" — order is everything.

About this Tool

An interactive Network+ exam prep tool for CompTIA N10-009 Objective 5.1 — the network troubleshooting methodology. Covers the 7 official steps, sub-actions under each, common anti-patterns, and the order-of-operations the exam tests heavily.

Built around process knowledge: knowing the steps in order, knowing what each one includes, and knowing what NOT to do at each one.
